Abstract

We present Latent Drifting, a novel approach for counterfactual medical image synthesis using diffusion models. Medical counterfactual generation is crucial for creating controlled variations in medical images that can help with diagnosis, treatment planning, and medical education. Our method addresses a critical limitation in current diffusion-based approaches that often struggle to maintain anatomical consistency while introducing clinically meaningful variations. By strategically manipulating the latent space trajectories during the diffusion process, Latent Drifting generates highly realistic counterfactual medical images that preserve patient-specific anatomical structures while modifying targeted pathological features. Our approach achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple medical imaging modalities including MRI, CT, and X-ray datasets. Experiments demonstrate that Latent Drifting produces counterfactual images that are both clinically plausible and useful for downstream tasks such as data augmentation for disease classification and segmentation models, improving their performance by up to 18% on limited training data.

Overview

Counterfactual medical image synthesis aims to generate "what-if" scenarios by altering specific aspects of medical images while preserving patient-specific anatomical structures. This is particularly valuable for medical applications where obtaining diverse pathological data is challenging. Our Latent Drifting approach leverages foundation diffusion models to create high-quality counterfactual medical images through:

  • A novel trajectory manipulation technique in the latent space of diffusion models
  • An anatomical consistency preservation mechanism that maintains patient-specific structures
  • A pathology-aware guidance method that enables controlled modification of disease features
  • A comprehensive evaluation framework for assessing the clinical utility of synthetic medical images

Method

Figure 1: Overview of the Latent Drifting framework for counterfactual medical image synthesis, showing the diffusion process with controlled trajectory manipulation.

Our Latent Drifting approach extends foundation diffusion models with specialized components for medical counterfactual generation:

  1. Anatomical Structure Encoder: A network that extracts patient-specific anatomical features to be preserved during synthesis
  2. Pathology Manipulation Module: A mechanism for controlled modification of disease-specific features in the latent space
  3. Drift Controller: A novel component that guides the diffusion trajectory to balance anatomical consistency and pathological modification
  4. Medical Plausibility Validator: A discriminator trained on real medical images to ensure clinical realism of generated counterfactuals

Results

Key findings:

  • Latent Drifting achieves 27.4% improvement in pathology realism scores compared to state-of-the-art methods
  • Our method maintains 92.3% anatomical consistency with original patient images while successfully modifying targeted pathological features
  • Models trained with our counterfactual augmentation show 18.2% improvement in disease classification accuracy on limited training data
  • Radiologist evaluation confirms the clinical plausibility of our synthesized counterfactuals with 86% acceptance rate
